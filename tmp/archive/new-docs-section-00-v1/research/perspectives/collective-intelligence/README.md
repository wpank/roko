## What This Lens Illuminates

Multi-agent AI systems are often designed by composing individual agents: make each agent
better, and the group improves. The c-factor evidence says this is wrong — or at least
incomplete. Group intelligence is not the sum of individual intelligences; it is an
emergent property of the group's **interaction structure**.

This lens makes visible:
- What structural properties of multi-agent Roko deployments predict collective performance.
- Why adding more agents may not improve collective performance (or may reduce it).
- What the analog of "social sensitivity" is for AI agents.
- How to measure c in a multi-agent system.
