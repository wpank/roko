## Intent

Introduce a conservative contextual bandit layer for decisions that now have stable observations and action identifiers.

## Implementation Requirements

- Identify the first decision points that have enough telemetry:
  - provider/model routing;
  - prompt/context section inclusion;
  - bidder budget share;
  - reviewer model choice, if present.
- Add a typed bandit policy interface with context features, action ids, reward observations, and safety bounds.
- Implement a simple initial strategy, such as Thompson sampling for bounded pass/fail rewards or epsilon-greedy behind a conservative flag.
- Use task type, crate/surface, role id, prior gate history, latency, retry count, and cost/token data as available features.
- Emit `PolicyUpdate` candidate records rather than silently mutating production policy.
- Add tests for action selection, reward update, cold start, disabled mode, and safety bounds.

## Acceptance

- Bandit decisions are structured, observable, and reversible.
- Cold-start and disabled behavior remain deterministic.
- Rewards are tied to gate outcomes and operational metrics.
- Promotion to active policy requires explicit candidate/admission handling.
