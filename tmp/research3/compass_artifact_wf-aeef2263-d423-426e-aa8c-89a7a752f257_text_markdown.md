## 4. Academic research validates the self-improving agent thesis

### Self-improvement is no longer theoretical

Three landmark papers demonstrate empirical, measurable self-improvement in coding agents. The **Darwin Gödel Machine** (Sakana AI/UBC, arXiv:2505.22954) improved SWE-bench performance from **20.0% → 50.0%** through iterative self-modification with Darwinian validation. **SICA** (University of Bristol, arXiv:2504.15228, ICLR 2025 workshop) achieved **17% → 53%** on SWE-bench Verified, demonstrating that scaffolding-only self-improvement (no weight updates) works for practical coding tasks. Most significantly, **HyperAgents** (Meta/UBC/Vector Institute, arXiv:2603.19461, ICLR 2026) introduced metacognitive self-modification — agents that improve not only task-solving behavior but the improvement mechanism itself. HyperAgents achieved cross-domain transfer from paper review + robotics to Olympiad math grading (imp@50 = 0.630 vs. 0.0 for human-designed baselines) and spontaneously developed persistent memory, performance tracking, and compute-aware planning.

The **GVU Framework** (arXiv:2512.02731) provides rigorous mathematical foundations: it proves a "Second Law of AGI Dynamics" (hallucination increases unless verification signal is sufficient) and derives a Variance Inequality prescribing conditions for stable self-improvement. Its actionable insight — **"strengthen the verifier, not the generator"** — directly validates architectures that invest in evaluation and context engineering rather than raw model scale. ICLR 2026 hosted a dedicated workshop on Recursive Self-Improvement, confirming the field has moved "from thought experiments to deployed systems."

### Context engineering is the highest-leverage intervention

**Meta-Harness** (Stanford/MIT, arXiv:2603.28052, March 2026) formalized what Andrej Karpathy articulated conceptually: an outer-loop system that searches over harness code — the code determining what information to store, retrieve, and present to an LLM — can produce a **6x performance gap** on the same benchmark. It achieved +7.7 points over state-of-the-art on text classification while using **4x fewer context tokens**, and ranked #1 among all Haiku 4.5 agents on TerminalBench-2. This directly validates the thesis that the thick layer of software around LLMs — the scaffolding, context management, and retrieval orchestration — is where sustainable competitive advantage lies. Karpathy's June 25, 2025 endorsement (2.3M views): *"Context engineering is the delicate art and science of filling the context window with just the right information for the next step."*

**RouteLLM** (UC Berkeley, ICLR 2025) demonstrated **2x+ cost reduction** without quality loss through intelligent model routing. **FrugalGPT** (Stanford, arXiv:2305.05176) achieved up to **98% cost reduction** via learned LLM cascading. Together, these validate the market for intelligent orchestration in production agent systems.

### The CoALA cognitive architecture framework

**CoALA** (Princeton, arXiv:2309.02427, TMLR 2024, 280+ citations) provides the foundational taxonomy for language agent design: modular memory (working + long-term episodic/semantic/procedural), structured action spaces (internal + external), and generalized decision cycles. It contextualizes modern agent architectures within the broader history of cognitive science (Soar, ACT-R) and identifies underexplored directions that Nunchi's Roko architecture directly addresses.

### Hyperdimensional computing bridges symbolic and neural AI

Nunchi's use of Binary Spatter Codes is grounded in mature academic foundations. **Kanerva (2009)** established that in ~10,000-dimensional spaces, random vectors are nearly orthogonal — the "blessing of dimensionality" — enabling noise-tolerant, compositionally structured representations via three operations: binding (XOR), bundling (majority rule), and permutation. **Kleyko et al. (ACM Computing Surveys, 2023)** surveyed the full HDC/VSA field across 959+ citations of Kanerva's work, documenting applications in NLP, edge computing, classification, robotics, and cognitive modeling.

Two 2025 papers bridge HDC to modern LLMs. The **Hyperdimensional Probe** (arXiv:2509.25045) demonstrated that VSA-based probing can extract interpretable, structured features from LLM internal representations — validated from GPT-2 to Llama 4 Scout (109B parameters). **"Attention as Binding"** (arXiv:2512.14709) reinterprets transformer self-attention as implementing approximate VSA: queries and keys define role spaces, values encode fillers, attention weights perform soft unbinding, and residual connections realize superposition. This paper proposes concrete architectural innovations — explicit binding/unbinding attention heads, hyperdimensional memory layers — offering an engineering roadmap for agents with more reliable symbolic reasoning.

### Consensus, stigmergy, and cognitive foundations

**Simplex BFT** (Chan and Pass, TCC 2023) provides the consensus layer for Korai's architecture: a partially synchronous BFT protocol tolerating f < n/3 Byzantine faults with simpler proofs and **3Δ view-change timeout** (vs. 6Δ for Tendermint). It is being adopted by Commonware and (with modifications) by Solana, validating practical applicability.

**Stigmergy** — indirect coordination through environmental traces — provides the theoretical foundation for Korai's decentralized agent coordination model. The lineage runs from Grassé (1959, termite nest-building), through Dorigo (1992, Ant Colony Optimization), to modern digital stigmergy in multi-agent RL systems (Xu et al., IEEE TNNLS 2022). On-chain stigmergy, where blockchain state serves as the shared medium, is an emerging paradigm for autonomous agent swarms.

**Complementary Learning Systems theory** (McClelland/McNaughton/O'Reilly, 1995; updated by Kumaran/Hassabis/McClelland, 2016) blueprints dual-system memory: a fast hippocampal store for rapid episodic capture and a slow neocortical store for gradual knowledge consolidation. DeepMind's DQN used CLS-inspired replay buffers; a 2025 position paper argued LLM agents specifically need episodic memory alongside parametric knowledge for long-term operation.

The **PAD emotional model** (Mehrabian & Russell, 1974) provides a compact 3D space (Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance) for representing agent emotional states, enabling emotion-modulated decision-making. Computational engines like WASABI and FAtiMA implement PAD for animated characters and social robots; a Nature 2024 paper proposed the first fully self-learning emotional framework for AI agents using PAD coordinates.

Compound has declined to ~$1.3–2.0B TVL with below-market yields (~2.6% APY).

### Pendle's success and structural limitations

Pendle Finance validated yield tokenization as a category: **$5.8B average TVL** (79% YoY increase), **$47.8B trading volume**, and $58B in fixed income provided. Its Boros product for funding rate derivatives achieved $5.5B trading volume in four months. However, the expiring yield futures model has fundamental constraints:

- **Liquidity fragmentation** across maturity dates creates thin pools and high slippage
- **Maturity-driven TVL cliffs** with sharp post-expiry drawdowns
- **YT time decay** toward zero at maturity (potential -100% loss instrument)
- **~70% of volume is speculation-driven**, with uncertain institutional sustainability
- **PENDLE token underperformed -62% YoY** despite strong protocol metrics

Notable predecessors — Element Finance ($32M raised, a16z/Polychain), Yield Protocol ($10M, Paradigm), Notional Finance ($10M) — all failed in this space while Pendle survived.

### Hyperliquid proves the perpetual infrastructure model

Hyperliquid's performance validates perpetual instruments as DeFi infrastructure: **$2.95T total 2025 volume**, **$6B TVL**, **70% DEX perpetuals market share**, and **$844M annual revenue**. HIP-3 (October 2025) enables **permissionless perpetual market creation** by staking 500K HYPE (~$25M), with builder-deployed open interest reaching $790M ATH by January 2026. Markets now include Tesla, gold, silver, and S&P 500 — bridging TradFi and DeFi. HIP-4 adds fully collateralized prediction/outcomes markets.

### The perpetual yield gap

**No major protocol has built perpetual (non-expiring) yield futures at scale.** Pendle uses expiring instruments; Boros uses fixed-term contracts. Rho Protocol processes $10B+ notional in interest rate swaps but remains niche. TermMax ($49M TVL) offers fixed-rate lending. The traditional interest rate swap market is **$600T+ in notional outstanding** (BIS) — the single largest financial market globally — with effectively **0% on-chain penetration**. This represents the structural gap Nunchi's Korai could address.

### TEE infrastructure is production-ready

Trusted Execution Environments, particularly **AWS Nitro Enclaves**, are production-proven in DeFi. **Turnkey** secures millions of wallets for Polymarket, Azura, and Moonshot using 5 specialized Rust-based enclave applications with 99.9% uptime and 50–100ms signing latency (50–100x faster than MPC). **Fireblocks** ($8B valuation) processes $10T+ in secured transfers using hybrid MPC+TEE. **Unichain** became the first L2 to build blocks inside a TEE via Rollup Boost. Over **50 teams** worldwide are building TEE-blockchain solutions. Marlin Protocol enables on-chain verification of AWS Nitro Enclave attestations — critical infrastructure for verifiable computation.

### A2A complements MCP for agent collaboration

Google's Agent-to-Agent protocol launched April 2025 with **50+ technology partners** and was donated to the Linux Foundation in June 2025, growing to **150+ organizations**. However, actual commerce volume remains early (~$28K daily real volume vs. narrative). Google's **Agent Payments Protocol (AP2)** launched with 60+ partners including Mastercard, PayPal, and Revolut. Visa launched its **Trusted Agent Protocol**. OpenAI disclosed a **4% transaction fee** for agentic checkout. Stablecoin transaction volumes grew from $450B to **$710B** in early 2025 with 35M unique wallets. Agentic commerce is projected to drive **~$3T in global GMV within five years** (Everest Group).

### Agent memory has reached an inflection point

Memory has emerged as the **critical differentiator** for production agents. A December 2025 survey paper established a unified taxonomy (episodic, semantic, procedural, working memory), and ICLR 2026 proposed a dedicated MemAgents workshop. **Mem0** raised a $24M Series A (October 2025) for hybrid vector+graph memory. **Zep/Graphiti** uses temporal knowledge graphs with sub-200ms retrieval, scoring 15 points higher than Mem0 on LongMemEval. **Letta (formerly MemGPT)** implements OS-inspired 3-tier memory. Graph memory has moved from experimental (2024) to production (2026). Self-improving agents via procedural memory represent a major research surge: MACLA (AAMAS 2026) achieved 82% cold-start capability with <5% LLM fallback after training.

## Conclusion: Nunchi occupies a genuine blue ocean

Five convergent forces create the investment case. First, the **self-improvement thesis is academically validated**: DGM, SICA, and HyperAgents prove that scaffolding-based self-improvement works, and Meta-Harness shows context engineering produces 6x performance gains — the exact capability Roko's cognitive architecture is designed to automate. Second, **blockchain-AI infrastructure is undersupplied**: the $700M–$1.1B market has no player combining cognitive agent runtimes with on-chain coordination, while a16z explicitly calls for agent wallets, KYA frameworks, and machine-scale payments. Third, **perpetual yield instruments represent a $600T addressable market** with zero on-chain penetration — Pendle's success proved yield tokenization works but its expiring model has structural limitations that a perpetual architecture can overcome. Fourth, **comparable valuations strongly support the ask**: AI infrastructure Series A rounds at $20–25M consistently achieve $76M–$200M valuations, even pre-revenue, and the "agentic" label adds a 40% premium. Fifth, **macro conditions are optimal**: pro-innovation regulation on both AI and crypto, accelerating VC deployment ($1.7B from a16z alone for AI infrastructure), enterprise demand outstripping supply (75% stuck at pilot), and a favorable exit environment with CoreWeave's successful IPO proving the model.

The key risk factors to address proactively include capital concentration at the mega-round level (which may crowd narrative away from Series A companies), the extreme volatility of crypto-AI tokens (Olas -99% from ATH, ASI -93%), pre-revenue status requiring clear path-to-market articulation, and the rapid commoditization of agent frameworks requiring Roko's Rust-native cognitive architecture and Korai's stigmergic coordination layer to demonstrate durable differentiation against well-funded alternatives.
