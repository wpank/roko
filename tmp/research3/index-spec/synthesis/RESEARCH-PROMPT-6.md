## What I need you to research

Round 6 stops asking "how do we ship the first 90 days." That is solved. Round 6 asks: **given the stack we are building (Korai chain + Roko runtime + ISFR benchmark + HDC + DKG + agent identity + work receipts + browser-verifiable proofs), what are the highest-asymmetric, exponentially-scaling, non-obvious bets we should be planning for years 2-5?**

Filter every candidate through five gates. A finding that fails any single gate should be rejected:

1. **Not in synthesis docs 01-22.** If it has been discussed before, do not surface it again. Read all 22 first.
2. **Exponential scaling mechanism.** Network effects, data flywheels, reputation graphs, cost-cliff dynamics, standards adoption (Metcalfe), or compounding knowledge. Linear-scaling opportunities are out of scope.
3. **Structural advantage from the Nunchi stack.** The opportunity must be substantially harder for Anthropic, OpenAI, Microsoft, Google, AWS, LangChain, or Temporal to reach. Specifically: HDC fingerprinting, on-chain receipts, DKG-private collaboration, agent-attested data, browser-verifiable proofs, methodology-as-code, or principal/delegate accountability must be load-bearing.
4. **$10B+ TAM ceiling.** Niches under $10B do not justify R&D opportunity cost. Justify the TAM with named comparables.
5. **First-mover decisive.** If this is "anyone can build this in six months," it is not asymmetric. The opportunity must reward the first credible operator and punish followers.

Be specific. Name companies, cite numbers, reference real markets, name the exact data flywheel, name the exact incumbent that cannot copy it, and name the year-by-year compounding mechanism.
