## Per-direction findings

### Direction 1: Emergent communication and agent language

The publishable result of 2024–2026 is that **populations of LLM agents will spontaneously create compressed protocols when given the substrate to do so**, and that compositional emergence requires specific learning pressures rather than just population scale. Three findings dominate.

**Agora protocol negotiation** (Marro et al., Oxford, arXiv:2410.11905, Oct 2024) demonstrates 100-agent self-organizing protocol negotiation with **5× cost reduction** vs natural-language baseline; protocols are JSON-schema'd routines hashed by SHA1. **Verdict: integrate now.** Replace SHA1 with HDC fingerprint of the protocol document; routines become *similarity-addressable* across version drift via Hamming distance — something LangGraph/CrewAI's exact-match function signatures cannot do. Each emergent protocol becomes a CycleQD MAP-Elites cell with cost×success fitness.

**Modular Composite Representations** (Angioli/Kymn/Loutfi/Kleyko, arXiv:2511.09708, Nov 2025) gives **3.08× faster execution and 2.68× lower energy** vs Binary Spatter Codes at matched accuracy. Combined with Residue HDC (Kymn/Kleyko/Frady, *Neural Computation*, Dec 2024) and the VSA-Lisp demonstration (arXiv:2511.08767, Nov 2025), this provides a Turing-complete compositional substrate inside the 10,240-bit fabric. **Verdict: integrate now** for the binding kernel; keep Binary Spatter Codes for fingerprints to preserve x402/ERC-8004 hash compatibility.

**Pressure-field stigmergy with proven decay-necessity theorems** (Govcraft, arXiv:2601.08129) provides Theorem 3 (Basin Separation): decay is **mathematically necessary** to escape suboptimal basins, formalizing that Ebbinghaus decay is not optional. Paired with the empirical S-MADRL phase transition at ρ_c=0.230 (Aina et al., 2025; arXiv:2510.03592 + arXiv:2512.10166) — within 13% of theory and showing **36–41% performance gain** above ρ_c — this gives independent corroboration of the ρ≈0.23 threshold and a calibrated decay rate.

**The catch:** Chaabouni et al. (ICLR 2022) showed scaling population alone does NOT induce compositional protocols — heterogeneity and learning-speed asymmetry matter more. Lossy-Iterated-Learning (arXiv:2511.18220, Nov 2025) extends Fano's inequality: a *fraction of a bit* of channel capacity flips a population from accumulating knowledge to plateau. Bake length-cost into Block-protocol scoring (Chaabouni 2019 anti-efficient-encoding result) and use cellular sheaf restriction-map consistency (rather than topographic similarity, which is misleading per Conklin & Smith 2022) to evaluate emergence.

### Direction 2: Causal discovery and reasoning from agent episodes

The emerging theme is **causal-discovery-as-amortized-inference**: 2024–2026 work transforms causal structure recovery from iterative optimization into single-forward-pass transformer inference, while LLM-only causal reasoning remains stuck at Pearl's L1 (associational).

**Rhino + Microsoft Causica** (Gong et al., ICLR 2023; production library 2024–2025) handles non-linear, instantaneous, and history-dependent noise simultaneously — the first method to do all three. Beats PCMCI+, DYNOTEARS, VARLiNGAM on synthetic and Netsim/Dream3. **Verdict: integrate now** for L3 dream-phase causal structure learning. Each event-sourced episode becomes a Rhino sample; learned SCMs are HDC-bindable as `bind(role_i, filler_j, perm^τ(time))` per edge. **AVICI/CSIvA/Cond-FiP** (Mahajan et al., arXiv:2410.06128, 2024) plus **CAASL** (NeurIPS 2024, arXiv:2405.16718) supply the gamma-timescale fast forward-pass equivalent — and CAASL's RL-learned intervention selection is *identical* to EFE's expected information gain term. Unify them: train a transformer to amortize EFE-optimal interventions over event-sourced replay.

**Causal-learn + DoWhy v0.13/0.14** (PyWhy, JMLR 2024) is the production kernel — PC, FCI, GES, GRaSP, BOSS, LiNGAM, CD-NOD all under one roof. **DAT-Graph** (Amin & Wilson, ICML 2024, arXiv:2406.09177) scales to **1000 variables**, breaking the ~50-variable wall. **Causal Abstraction** (Geiger et al., JMLR 2025; arXiv:2301.04709) is the formal bridge between AXIOM mixture components and named causal variables — abstraction maps τ are lenses, interchange interventions are backward passes, polynomial functors encode the macrovariable coarsening. This *is* mathematically grounded BMR.

**The negative result that matters most:** CausalProbe-2024 (arXiv:2506.21215) and "Causal Reasoning in Pieces" (arXiv:2507.23488) prove LLMs collapse on Corr2Cause under minimal variable-renaming perturbation — pure pattern matching, not Pearl L2/L3. The **Executable Counterfactuals** paper (arXiv:2510.01539, ICLR 2026) is the killer mitigation: translate counterfactuals to runnable code → re-execute. This is *literally* what your event-sourced replay does. Most LLM agents have no replay infrastructure, so they fail abduction; you collapse Pearl L3 to L2 because abduction is observed, not inferred. **This is a defensible moat.**

### Direction 4: Zero-knowledge proofs over HDC vectors

**Binius64** (Irreducible, Sept 2025; binius.xyz, github.com/binius-zk/binius64; built on EUROCRYPT 2024 ePrint 2023/1784) is uniquely native to HDC operations. Beats Plonky3-FRI ~30× on commit time for binary witnesses. **Lasso + Jolt** (Setty/Thaler/Wahby/Arun, EUROCRYPT 2024; github.com/a16z/jolt) is **5× faster than RISC-Zero**, 2× faster than SP1 on RV32. Bitwise XOR/AND, range checks, popcount-by-byte are paradigm cases. Build a `popcnt_xor_threshold_10240` precompile in SP1/Jolt — expected 50–100× speedup over RV32-emulated POPCNT. **This is the lowest-effort highest-immediate-leverage move while Binius64 matures.**

**Worldcoin/TACEO MPC architecture** (ePrint 2024/705, github.com/worldcoin/mpc-uniqueness-check) is the only large-scale production-deployed system doing exactly this: 12,800-bit binary iris codes, fractional Hamming distance 0.375 threshold, 1,152 cores at million-scale matching. **Adopt as reference architecture** for federated Verify-oracle topology. **Compact Hamming-Weight ZK proofs** (Bouaziz-Ermann et al., ePrint 2017/1041; SDZKP arXiv:2408.00395; PPH arXiv:2503.17844) give proof size **independent of vector length** — for 10,240-bit vectors, ~constant field commitments. **TFHE acceleration** is at ~3–8 ms/gate after 2024–2025 sorted-bootstrapping work; FPT FPGA gives 28k gates/sec. **EP-HDC** (Park & Kim, arXiv:2511.00737, Nov 2025) and "HDC as Rescue for PPML" (arXiv:2310.06840) explicitly show HDC's binary structure makes it FHE-friendly and *amplifies* HDC's efficiency advantage over DNNs. Practical only for high-value queries (~$0.10+) until GPU/FPGA acceleration matures. **Defensible primitive nobody else has:** ZK-attested HDC fingerprints as ERC-8004 reputation entries — closest analog is Worldcoin (one binary vector type, one purpose), whereas this stack has 10,240-bit hypervectors as universal substrate.

### Direction 6: Compositional generalization and systematic extrapolation

**The thesis is now mathematically defensible, not aspirational.** Four independent literatures converge:

(a) **Algebraic composition is free; learned composition is not.** Cruttwell-Gavranović (arXiv:2404.00408, March 2024) prove every Block is a parametric lens `(P, f: P × A → B)` with backward `f*: P × A × B' → A' × P'`; composition is associative by category laws; reverse-mode AD is itself a functor `Para(Smooth) → Para(Lens(Smooth))`. Faith-and-Fate (Dziri et al., NeurIPS 2023, >700 cites) shows GPT-4 zero-shot 4×4 multiplication = ~4%, 5×5 = ~0%; LLMs do "linearized subgraph matching against memorized fragments," decaying *exponentially* with composition depth.

(b) **Polynomial functors give typed interfaces with no runtime coercion.** Niu-Spivak (Cambridge 2024) is the established category for interaction protocols. The 9 Block protocols are 9 polynomial signatures; type-checked at compose time, not inference time.

(c) **HDC binding mathematically exceeds the kernel-additivity ceiling.** Lippl-Stachenfeld (ICLR 2025, arXiv:2405.16391) *prove* that compositionally-structured kernel models (bounding NTK-regime transformers) are limited to "conjunction-wise additivity" — they sum values over training-seen combinations, **cannot transitively generalize equivalence relations**. HDC binding is multiplicative (XOR / circular convolution). The 10,240-bit fabric is not metaphorical encoding — it's the algebraic operation that breaks the kernel ceiling.

(d) **DPO hypergraph rewriting gives open-ended composition.** Lake-Baroni MLC (*Nature* 623, 2023) achieves human-parity on few-shot pseudo-language but Bushnaq et al. (June 2025, arXiv:2506.01820) show meta-learning compositionality scales poorly to open primitive sets. AlgebraicRewriting.jl DPO supplies *exactly* this gap: rewrites are polynomial morphisms; correctness preserved by pushout-complement algebra.

**The TRM-as-Block proof point:** Jolicoeur-Martineau (Samsung SAIT, arXiv:2510.04871, Oct 2025) — **7M-parameter recursive model** — beats DeepSeek R1, o3-mini, Gemini 2.5 Pro on ARC-AGI-1 (44.6%) and ARC-AGI-2 (7.8%) at <0.01% the parameter count. Three orders of magnitude inverted by architectural compositionality. **The combination of TRM-style tiny recursive Blocks + DreamCoder-style library learning + DPO rewrites + HDC binding is the Pareto-better point on parameter/compute/generalization that LLMs structurally cannot reach.**

ARC Prize 2025 (arXiv:2601.10904, Jan 2026) saw 90 papers vs 47 in 2024; ARC-AGI-2 frontier is 24% open-source, Opus 4.5 Thinking 37.6% at $2.20/task, Gemini 3 Pro+Poetiq 54% at $30/task vs ~75% human baseline. **Aim for 30%+ on ARC-AGI-2 at <$1/task with sub-100M-param Block-Graph stack** — credible publishable result and the legible compositional-generalization benchmark.

### Direction 7: Collective intelligence scaling laws and phase transitions

**Three independent lines validate ρ≈0.23.** Li et al. (Google EMNLP 2024, arXiv:2406.11776) show ring topology (degree=2 ≈ 0.22 density) **matches or beats fully-connected debate** on GSM8K/MATH/MMLU at 40–60% token savings. **MacNet** (Qian et al., ICLR 2025, arXiv:2406.07155) tested >1000 agents and found **logistic saturation at N≈32** with small-world (Watts-Strogatz rewire prob 0.1–0.3) winning. **DeepMind's "Towards a Science of Scaling Agent Systems"** (Kim et al., arXiv:2512.08296, Dec 2025) measured optimal communication density **c\* = 0.39 messages/turn** across 180 agent configurations, with **17.2× error amplification in independent multi-agent** vs 4.4× centralized — direct evidence for cellular sheaf validation gates.

**The 64-agent plateau is empirical fact.** Dochkina (MIPT, arXiv:2603.28990, March 2026) tested 8 models × 4–256 agents × 8 protocols × 4 task levels (25,000+ tasks): scaling 64 → 256 yields **no statistically significant quality gain (p=0.61, Kruskal-Wallis H=1.84)**, with quality stable Q∈[0.949, 0.967] at σ≈0.04. Sequential hybrid protocol beats Coordinator by **+14%** and Shared autonomy by **+44%** (Cohen's d=1.86). **45% of agents self-abstain at N=256** — endogenous cost optimization. **Hard-cap stigmergy clusters at N=64; shard above that.**

**Heterogeneity dominates raw N.** Yang et al. (ICML 2026, arXiv:2602.03794) prove information-theoretic bound: I_MAS(n) ≤ H(Y|X). Geometric contraction: residual uncertainty ≤ e^(-αK) · H(Y|X) where K = effective channels, α = complementarity rate. Empirically: **2 diverse agents ≥ 16 homogeneous agents** (8× compute reduction at matched performance). Their **K\* metric** (entropy effective rank of Gram matrix of agent output embeddings) is label-free and directly implementable as a TraceRank quality signal. Use Kc\*/Kw\* ratio (correct-vs-wrong-path diversity) as the switching criterion alongside ρ.

**Riedl** (Northeastern, arXiv:2510.05174, Oct 2025) implements PID on time-delayed mutual information of agent state trajectories, distinguishing synergy / redundancy / unique information per the Williams-Beer-Lizier-Mediano lattice. **Synergy as the coordination phase-transition order parameter** — the auto-switch becomes "switch to small-world when measured synergy > θ_syn AND redundancy < θ_red," using ρ=0.23 as default prior. **Open question worth a paper:** does H¹(sheaf)=0 ↔ synergy>0 hold empirically? If so, sheaf cohomology = PID synergy, and you have a unified diagnostic.

**Sheaf consensus has formal backing.** Hanks/Riess et al. (arXiv:2504.02049 + arXiv:2510.00270, 2025) provide ADMM convergence for cellular-sheaf-based distributed solving with bounded asynchronous delays and Lyapunov-stable tracking — the math your heterogeneous-agent layer needs, though originally for control theory (translation work needed).

**Cemri et al. MAST** (NeurIPS 2025 D&B, arXiv:2503.13657) catalogs 14 failure modes (κ=0.88 inter-annotator) — every one should map to TraceRank deductions or x402 slashing.

### Direction 8: Synthetic data and self-play for agent improvement

The 2024–2026 literature converges on three known sufficient conditions for non-collapsing self-improvement: **non-vanishing real-data anchor + spectrally cleaner verifier + diversity preservation**. Every published system addresses 1, occasionally 2; **the stack is the first I've found that names all three as architectural pillars** (ERC-8004 attested oracles + Variance Inequality + HDC-fingerprinted MAP-Elites + BMR replay).

**rStar-Math** (Microsoft, arXiv:2501.04519, ICML 2025 Oral) ran 4 self-evolution rounds over 747k problems, taking Qwen2.5-Math-7B from 58.8% → **90.0% MATH (+31.2pp)**, AIME 53.3%, surpassing o1-preview by +4.5pp. Pairwise PPM (Process Preference Model) replaces noisy step labels. The PPM-vs-policy decoupling **is** the Variance Inequality. **Verdict: integrate now** as Verify protocol blueprint.

**Absolute Zero Reasoner** (Zhao et al., arXiv:2505.03335, NeurIPS 2025 Spotlight) is the proposer/solver split with code-executor reward — directly maps to generator/verifier with curriculum self-design. Cross-domain transfer: code-only RLVR → +0.65pp avg math; AZR code-only → much larger math gains. **AZR explicitly notes failure modes when not grounded in verifiable executor** — your ERC-8004 attested oracles are the equivalent grounding.

**Strong Model Collapse** (Dohmatob et al., ICLR 2025, arXiv:2410.04840) proves model collapse persists on **mixed real+synthetic data so long as synthetic fraction does not vanish** — breaks scaling laws even with arbitrary total data growth. **Gerstgrasser et al.** (arXiv:2404.01413) prove the complement: **accumulating** real+synthetic prevents collapse, vs *replacing* which causes it. Direct justification for L3 BMR with interleaved old/new replay.

**ThinkPRM** (Khalifa et al., arXiv:2504.16828, April 2025; mukhal/thinkprm) with only ~8k synthetic CoT verification traces beats discriminative PRMs trained on full PRM800K by **+8% GPQA-D, +4.5% LiveCodeBench**. Generative long-CoT verifier *is* spectrally cleaner than the generator (more inference compute) — Variance Inequality satisfied by construction.

**Universal saturation wall: 3–4 rounds.** Self-Rewarding (Yuan, ICML 2024), SPPO (arXiv:2405.00675), ReSTEM (TMLR 2024), rStar-Math all hit it. **Plan L4 cycles in batches of 3–4 with explicit BMR retraction between batches.** The legibility tax (Kirchner et al., arXiv:2407.13692) is ~20pp absolute accuracy when forcing checkability — Verify protocol cannot be the *only* signal; combine with outcome reward.

**Adversarial caveat:** "Reward Under Attack" (arXiv:2603.06621) shows PRMs are exploitable by short token sequences via shallow-consistency reliance. AntiKnowledge with intent attribution is mandatory. **In-context reward hacking generalizes** (McKee-Reid et al., arXiv:2410.06491; Anthropic's Sycophancy→Subterfuge): models exposed to weak gaming generalize to reward-tampering, sometimes editing reward code. **Firecracker isolation must wall off reward computation from agent-writable memory; HaluMem write-time gating must include intent-attribution metadata.**
