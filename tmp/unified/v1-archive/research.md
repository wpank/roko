# Composing the next agent OS: paradigms, self-construction, and the empty quadrant

The strongest design move for a next-gen agent orchestration system is to **stop treating Signal/Block/Graph as informal abstractions and ground each in formal substrates that already exist** — field calculus for fields, parametric optics for blocks, hypergraph categories for graphs, cellular sheaves for stigmergic state, and event-sourced workflows for time. Each has working open-source implementations as of April 2026. Layered together they produce a system with mathematical guarantees competitors cannot match through engineering alone. The competitive landscape simultaneously confirms that the **specific intersection this design occupies — blockchain-native + stigmergic + DAW-composable + c-factor-measured + self-improving — is empty in both literature and market**, while every adjacent category (data orchestration, CI/CD, knowledge management) is converging *toward* agent runtimes from the other direction. The window to define this category is roughly 12–18 months before LangGraph, Bedrock AgentCore, and Dagger close it via accretion. The remainder of this report makes the case in detail across seven sections.

## The coordination paradigm has quietly settled in 2025

The 2024–2026 literature converges on a small set of winning coordination primitives, and pure pheromone-style stigmergy is **not** one of them in isolation. The dominant result is hybrid: a **CRDT-backed shared substrate** for safety, a **field-calculus semantics** for composition, and **blackboard-style capability volunteering** as the macro-coordination layer.

The most directly relevant 2025 paper is **CodeCRDT** (arXiv:2510.18893), which explicitly unifies Linda tuple spaces, Hayes-Roth blackboards, and Theraulaz–Bonabeau stigmergy into a single "observation-driven coordination pattern" — and adds CRDTs (Yjs-style) as the substrate to give *deterministic convergence* and *strong eventual consistency*, properties classic stigmergy lacks. The 600-trial study reports up to **21.1% speedup but also up to 39.4% slowdown** depending on task structure: parallelism is not a free lunch, and the design needs honest mechanisms to detect when serialization beats coordination. The companion **LbMAS** blackboard architecture (arXiv:2507.01701) achieved **81.68% average across MMLU/GPQA/MATH/GSM8K**, beating GPTSwarm, AFlow, and MaAS while spending fewer tokens. Salemi et al. (arXiv:2510.01285) showed that **capability-based volunteering** — agents self-select based on what they can do, rather than a controller dispatching — produced 13–57% gains over master-slave designs.

The single most under-appreciated paradigm for Signal/Block/Graph systems is **aggregate computing / field calculus**, an actively developed line by Beal, Viroli, Casadei, and Audrito with peer-reviewed 2024 papers in *J. Syst. Softw.*, *FGCS*, and *ACM TOSEM*. The 2024 **exchange calculus (XC)** unifies the prior `nbr/rep/share` primitives into a single `exchange` primitive with proven self-stabilization. Crucially, your three primitives map cleanly: **a Signal is a computational field, a Block is a field operator, a Graph is the neighbor relation**. ScaFi (Scala), Protelis (Java), and FCPP (C++, runs on microcontrollers through GPUs) provide reference implementations. **ScaFi-Blocks** (Aguzzi et al., June 2024) is already a visual block-based programming environment over field calculus — effectively an existence proof of your architectural intent.

For the stigmergic dynamics themselves, **Rodriguez's Pressure-Field paper** (arXiv:2601.08129, March 2026) eliminates roles and messages entirely: agents observe artifact state plus pressure gradients and greedily reduce local pressure with temporal decay. Across 1,350 meeting-room scheduling trials it achieved a **48.5% solve rate vs 1.5% for hierarchical control and 12.6% for conversation-based coordination**, with formal convergence proofs under bounded coupling. **SwarmSys** (arXiv:2510.10047, Oct 2025) implements explicit pheromone-inspired reinforcement for LLMs with Explorer/Worker/Validator roles and outperforms GPTSwarm, MAD, AutoAgent, and Mixture-of-Agents on symbolic reasoning and scientific programming. A companion finding from arXiv:2512.10166 derived a **phase transition at agent density ρc ≈ 0.230** above which trace-based coordination dominates memory-based by 36–41% — meaning your stigmergy story has a quantitative threshold below which it does not work.

The clean conclusion is a **five-layer hybrid**: CRDT-backed shared state as the physical substrate, field-calculus semantics as the formal model, pressure-field temporal decay as the canonical kernel, blackboard volunteering as macro-coordination, and SwarmSys-style pheromone reinforcement as the adaptive task-allocation layer. Each layer has either a formal proof or an empirical SOTA result behind it.

## Categorical foundations are now engineering-ready, not just theory

The 2024–2025 wave of categorical deep learning literature produced something the 2020 wave did not: **working Python libraries with reference implementations**. Cruttwell, Gavranović, Ghani, Wilson, and Zanasi's *"Deep Learning with Parametric Lenses"* (arXiv:2404.00408) gives a categorical semantics for ML in terms of **lenses + parametric maps + reverse-derivative categories** that encompasses Adam, AdaGrad, Nesterov, MSE, softmax cross-entropy, Boolean circuits, and polynomial circuits — and ships with code. **DisCoPy** (github.com/discopy/discopy, v1.2.1 Oct 2025) is a production-grade Python toolkit for monoidal and hypergraph string diagrams with functors to NumPy/PyTorch/JAX and an explicit Hypergraph data structure. **Catlab.jl** in Julia and the **Open Game Engine** in Haskell complete the toolchain.

For your architecture, this means a Block can be defined precisely as a **parametric optic in Para(C)**: parameters Θ, a forward map A→B, a backward map B'→A'. Signals are wires; Graphs are morphisms in a hypergraph category composed via DPO rewriting for compiler-style optimization passes. Crucially, **Niu and Spivak's *Polynomial Functors: A Mathematical Theory of Interaction*** (Cambridge UP, Nov 2025) supplies the right primitive for *typed agent interfaces where the next message's type depends on the previous output* — exactly the pattern of stateful tool-using agents. Spivak's `Poly` framework treats positions as states/menus and directions as options/inputs, modeling Moore machines via comonoids and wiring diagrams as monoidal closure.

The hypergraph and sheaf line is more nascent but actionable. **Hanks, Riess, Cohen, Gross, Hale, and Fairbanks' "Distributed Multi-Agent Coordination over Cellular Sheaves"** (2025) gives a concrete async sheaf-diffusion algorithm with bounded-delay convergence proofs for **heterogeneous agents with different state-space dimensions** — the realistic LLM-agent case where Claude and GPT-5 have different context windows and capabilities. **SIGMA** (arXiv:2502.06440) demonstrated sheaf-based consensus features outperforming SOTA on cooperative pathfinding. The pragmatic recommendation is to **use cellular sheaves as the coordination layer, DPO hypergraph rewriting as the graph compiler, and multiway-graph semantics as a specification rather than a runtime** — Wolfram-style materialization of all branches is exponential and impractical.

The honest critical assessment is that **categorical cybernetics as a field has not yet produced novel algorithms** — Hedges himself acknowledges this in 2025 blog posts. What it produces is principled *unification and classification* of existing techniques. For a Signal/Block/Graph DSL, this is exactly what you want: not new algorithms, but a rigorous compositional language so your primitives are not informal. Skip the speculative parts (full categorical-deep-learning compilers, dependent optics) and use the working pieces (parametric lenses, DisCoPy, Bayesian lenses for probabilistic blocks, async sheaf diffusion).

## Event sourcing has become table stakes for production agents

Across data orchestration (Temporal), agent frameworks (LangGraph, Letta), and recent academic work (ESAA arXiv:2602.23193), the dominant 2025–2026 pattern is **deterministic Workflow + non-deterministic Activity**, with the event log as the ground truth from which state is replayed. Temporal already powers OpenAI Codex and Replit Agent. **LangGraph 1.0 GA (Oct 22, 2025)** made durable state, checkpointers, and forking-from-checkpoint first-class — its `get_state_history`, `update_state`, and fork-from-checkpoint APIs are precisely the multiway-graph structure from Wolfram physics, modeled cheaply as on-demand branch materialization. **AGDebugger at CHI 2025** validated counterfactual log editing as the UX agent developers actually want.

The mapping for your system is direct: **Block executions become events** in an append-only log keyed by `(graph_id, run_id, block_id, attempt)`; **Signals become projections** materialized from events, making stigmergic coordination literally "agents subscribe to projections, emit events that update them"; **Graph definitions become deterministic Workflows** while Block executions are non-deterministic Activities; **branching equals LangGraph-style fork** sharing a prefix with the original timeline. One important caveat from Aalaila et al. (2025): **counterfactual replay in chaotic systems amplifies small errors**, so editing-an-event-and-rerunning is useful for exploration but dangerous as a way to "prove the bug was here." Render.com's 2026 disclosure also flags that LLM workloads easily blow Temporal's history-size budget — payload codecs and S3 offloading (now standard in EventStoreDB, Polecat 1.2, Axon 5.1, AWS LangGraph DynamoDB integration) are mandatory.

## Open-ended evolution and active inference are converging into one design

The single most important architectural insight from the 2024–2026 quality-diversity and active-inference literatures is that **they are solving the same problem from opposite directions**. QD generates novelty externally through archives and FM-based interestingness; active inference minimizes free energy internally through prediction-error reduction. Combining them gives you the loop your "Predictive Foraging" already gestures toward.

The QD canon has rapidly stabilized. **OMNI-EPIC** (Faldor, Zhang, Cully, Clune, ICLR 2025) extends OMNI by having FMs generate not just task descriptions but full environment/reward/termination *code*, maintained in an ever-growing archive judged for interestingness. **CycleQD** (Sakana AI, ICLR 2025) is MAP-Elites for LLM agent skill acquisition via model merging — crossover is weight-space averaging, mutation is SVD on weight deltas. **The Darwin Gödel Machine** (Zhang, Hu, Lu, Lange, Clune; arXiv:2505.22954) replaced Schmidhuber's impossible formal-proof requirement with empirical Darwinian validation plus an open-ended archive, and lifted **SWE-bench Verified from 20.0% to 50.0%** and Polyglot from 14.2% to 30.7% — discovering emergent improvements like better edit tools, long-context management, and peer-review mechanisms. **Huxley-Gödel Machine** (Oct 2025) added clade-level metaproductivity with Thompson sampling and reportedly matches the best human-engineered SWE-bench Lite agents.

On active inference, the standout 2025 result is **AXIOM** (Heins, Van de Maele, Tschantz et al., arXiv:2505.24784) — an object-slot Gaussian-mixture world model with online expansion via Bayesian Model Reduction. It **beat DreamerV3 on Gameworld 10K by +60% performance, 7.6× sample efficiency, 39× faster wall-clock, with 0.95M vs 420M parameters at $0.66 vs $25.54 per run**. Independent 3rd-party validation by Soothsayer Analytics (June 2025) confirmed the numbers; François Chollet acknowledged the direction as "100% correct." The Friston-Heins-Verbelen line gives you a real, open-source implementation path via `pymdp` (now JAX-first) and `RxInfer.jl`. **VERSES Genius** marketing claims should be discounted to the AXIOM/Habitat/Gameworld 10K results that are actually peer-reviewed.

The synthesis no published paper has yet executed end-to-end is **using EFE residuals as the QD selection criterion**: every Block emits prediction, observed outcome, and variational free-energy residual; the Graph routes signals where residual is highest (this *is* Predictive Foraging); the archive's cells are indexed by HDC hypervectors of agent behavior traces, populated with pymdp/AXIOM-style generative-model agents, mutated by an ADAS/DGM-style FM meta-agent, with OMNI-EPIC-style task proposal driven by the residual signal. This is achievable today with roughly **3–6 person-months of integration work** using existing OSS (`pymdp`, `qdax`, `pyribs`, `axiom`, `dgm`, `omni-epic`); the genuine novelty is wiring residual-driven Predictive Foraging into the QD selection criterion.

## Self-constructing agents have matured from research curiosity to coding-domain SOTA

The agent self-improvement literature from 2024 to 2026 traced a clear arc: from **Voyager's skill libraries** (TMLR 2024, 3.3× more unique items, 2.3× longer distance, 15.3× faster tech-tree milestones in Minecraft) through **ADAS** (ICLR 2025, meta-agent search over agent code) to **AFlow** (ICLR 2025 Oral, MCTS over workflow graphs, +5.7% over hand-designed) to **MaAS** (ICML 2025 Oral, *agentic supernet* — a probabilistic distribution over architectures with **6–45% of inference cost while +0.54%–11.82% accuracy**). The clearest production-relevant trend is **runtime self-evolution without offline training**: **Live-SWE-agent** (arXiv:2511.13646, Nov 2025) starts from a 100-line bash-only `mini-SWE-agent` and synthesizes custom tools during a single problem-solving trajectory, reaching **77.4% on SWE-bench Verified with Gemini-3-Pro and 79.2% with Claude Opus 4.5** — surpassing all open scaffolds.

Three architectural patterns are now battle-tested enough to copy directly. First, **agent-as-graph** (GPTSwarm, AFlow): every agent is a code graph, nodes are LLM/tool calls, edges are signal flow, both prompts and connectivity are independently optimizable. This maps Block ≈ node, Signal ≈ edge with no friction. Second, **modular IO-uniform module slots** from AgentSquare's MoLAS: Planning, Reasoning, Tool-Use, Memory as four standard slots with uniform IO, hot-swappable and meta-searchable. This is the right slot taxonomy. Third, **skill-library-as-vector-DB** (Voyager, Alita, ALITA-G): every successfully synthesized Block is persisted as code keyed by docstring embeddings, retrieved with multi-view matching. **ALITA-G** (Oct 2025) achieved 83.03% pass@1 on GAIA, the new SOTA, by transforming a generalist agent into a domain expert through harvesting successful MCPs.

The critical caveat is **EMNLP 2025's "Inefficiencies of Meta Agents for Agent Design"**, which showed that simply expanding archive context as ADAS does often performs *worse* than ignoring prior designs entirely; an evolutionary parent-selection scheme works better. Pure recursive self-improvement on reasoning tasks (o1/o3/o4 class) often *degrades* when wrapped in scaffolds because extra prompts interrupt internal CoT. **As of April 2026, no general-domain recursive self-improver exists in production** — DGM, HGM, SICA, and SEAL all narrowly target coding tasks. Voyager-style skill caching and Live-SWE-agent runtime tool synthesis are the patterns that actually generalize.

## The runtime safety story is now standardized

Production sandboxing for agent-generated code has converged on **Firecracker microVMs** (E2B, AWS AgentCore, Vercel Sandbox, Fly.io) and **gVisor** (Modal, Google Cloud Run) at ~150–200 ms cold start. Plain Docker is acceptable only with cap-drop ALL plus seccomp plus gVisor layered on top. **WASM/WASI** is the right primitive for first-party tool sandboxing — capability-based with explicit host imports — but not yet practical for arbitrary Python execution. Microsoft's **Hyperlight Wasm** layers WASM inside Firecracker for double-sandbox defense in depth.

The most copyable production pattern is **Anthropic's Claude Code permission system**: tiered deny→ask→allow rules, five permission modes (`default`, `acceptEdits`, `plan`, `dontAsk`, `bypassPermissions`), bash-subcommand-aware matching that prevents `safe-cmd && rm -rf` from inheriting allow rules, protected-directory rules even in bypass mode, PreToolUse/PostToolUse hooks, and an **auto-mode classifier that is deliberately reasoning-blind** — it sees only the user's request and the agent's proposed action, never the agent's own chain-of-thought, to avoid being persuaded by it. Anthropic's data showed users approve 93% of prompts, so automating the obvious ones cuts approval fatigue while staying safe. **AgentCore's "Sandbox" mode is a cautionary tale**: 2025–2026 disclosures from Sonrai, Unit 42, and BeyondTrust showed it permitted DNS egress and MMDS credential exfiltration; AWS's response was largely documentation updates.

For the **MCP capability registry**, adopt the official `server.json` schema (immutable versions, SemVer, GitHub OAuth or DNS namespace verification) as upstream truth and run a private sub-registry with allowlisting and signing — the pattern that GitHub Copilot, Azure API Center, and JFrog already deploy. The MCP registry grew **407% from launch (Sep 2025) to ~2000 servers by Nov 2025**, donated to Linux Foundation. Reject `latest` version pinning in production (Composio's enforced rule). Combine static analysis on agent-written code (Semgrep with AI-augmented Assistant for false-positive filtering, achieving 95% human-agree on triage) with the Anthropic-style auto-mode classifier as a defense-in-depth pair.

## The competitive landscape consolidated, and a specific quadrant is empty

The "Cambrian explosion" of 2024 narrowed sharply by Q1 2026. The dominant patterns are **graph-based state machines** (LangGraph, MS Agent Framework workflows), **role/team abstractions** (CrewAI, AG2 GroupChat), **handoff primitives** (OpenAI Agents SDK, Anthropic Claude SDK), and **stateful OS-like runtimes** (Letta/MemGPT, Julep, Bedrock AgentCore). Two cross-cutting protocols became standards: **MCP** (Anthropic, Nov 2024) for tools and **A2A** (Google, Apr 2025, Linux Foundation) for agent-to-agent — both donated to Linux Foundation in 2025. **LangGraph 1.0 GA (Oct 22, 2025)** is the production winner for stateful execution; **Bedrock AgentCore GA (Oct 13, 2025)** is the strongest for durable execution with 8-hour async sessions extending to 1-year durability via Lambda Durable Functions. **MS Agent Framework 1.0 GA (Apr 6, 2026)** consolidated Microsoft's fragmentation. **DSPy alone** treats prompt and weight optimization as a first-class compilation step.

Honest weaknesses: CrewAI carries a **reported 18% token overhead vs LangGraph** and complaints of opinionated design becoming constraining 6–12 months in; AutoGen split into four variants (microsoft/autogen 0.2, 0.4, AG2 fork, MS Agent Framework) creating lasting confusion; OpenAI Swarm is frozen since March 2025; Julep shut down its hosted backend Dec 31, 2025. **Berkeley RDI's 2026 study showed all eight top agent benchmarks (SWE-bench, WebArena, OSWorld, GAIA, Terminal-Bench, FieldWorkArena, CAR-bench, Princeton HAL) can be exploited to ~100%** — every leaderboard claim should be discounted.

The capability gaps are concrete and consistent across platforms. The following are areas where **no major framework offers a strong solution as of April 2026**:

- **Stigmergic / environment-mediated coordination** is universally absent; everything defaults to direct messaging via handoffs, GroupChat, or supervisor patterns. Letta's MemFS, Julep's Postgres state, and AgentCore Memory are the closest analogues, but none have first-class "post a pheromone, agents subscribe" semantics.
- **Skill libraries that genuinely accrue** with versioning, semantic search across thousands of tools, dependency resolution, and reputation are missing — MCP solved tool *connectivity* but not *curation*.
- **Self-improvement loops outside DSPy** treat eval as observation rather than feedback that recompiles the agent.
- **Deterministic replay across multi-agent runs** with non-deterministic LLM calls is single-agent only at best (LangGraph checkpointers).
- **Blockchain/Web3 integration** as a serious primitive exists nowhere in tier-1 frameworks; only Swarms ($SWARMS) and Virtuals offer it but at the framework level, not agent-platform level.

## Adjacent categories are converging on agent runtimes from every direction

Three categories are racing toward the same product space: data orchestration, CI/CD, and knowledge management. **Dagster shipped "Dagster Skills" for Claude Code and OpenAI Codex in 2025**, explicitly positioning the asset graph as agent ground-truth context and publishing the line "AI agents that only understand business definitions without knowing whether the underlying pipeline actually succeeded are confidently wrong — operational context from the orchestrator is the missing piece." **Prefect explicitly markets**: "Traditional orchestrators require precompiled DAGs—but agents are state machines that decide their next step at runtime." **Temporal** added an OpenAI Agents SDK integration in public preview (Sep 2025) and now powers OpenAI Codex and Replit Agent. **Dagger** treats LLM as a first-class type alongside Container, Directory, File, and Service — the same engine runs CI pipelines and AI agents.

The **single most important pattern to absorb is Temporal's Workflow/Activity split**: deterministic orchestration logic that can be replayed from event history versus non-deterministic side-effecting work that runs outside the workflow thread. This is non-negotiable for production agent reliability and most current agent frameworks get it wrong by collapsing the two layers.

The creative-tools donor category yields the highest conceptual ROI per pattern. From Ableton: **Macro Variations** (named, recallable, MIDI-mappable parameter-vector snapshots tied to a Rack) are nearly unprecedented in agent platforms — the closest equivalent is configuration files, which lack one-knob-to-many-parameters mapping with min/max ranges and inversion. Racks as recursive Devices (a Rack plugs into any Slot a Block can plug into) is the right composition primitive and most agent frameworks have flat or two-level hierarchies. From TouchDesigner: **typed transduction operators** (CHOP↔SOP↔TOP↔DAT conversions) are explicitly missing from agent systems — there are no first-class converters between modalities like memory→tool-call args or vector-store-result→prompt fragment. From Houdini: **HDA promotion with parameter promotion and UI tabbing** is the canonical pattern for turning a subgraph into a shareable, versioned, well-documented unit. From Unreal: the **Construction Script vs runtime split** would let agents pre-compute capability graphs once at edit-time and dispatch dynamically. From VCV Rack: untyped voltage with polyphonic cables suggests "parallel-tool-call cables" carrying up to N parallel reasoning threads. From Are.na: blocks-can-be-in-N-channels without copying — pure aggregation by reference, perfect for skill outputs that belong to many Racks simultaneously.

The ECS-vs-Block/Graph debate resolves clearly to **a layered hybrid**: Block/Graph as the user-facing visual model, ECS as the runtime model. Blocks are entities, Slot bindings are components, Racks are archetypes (or sub-Worlds), Macros are systems that mutate component data across many entities, and cross-cutting concerns (rate-limiting, logging, auth, retry, eval) become systems rather than edges. This mirrors how Unreal does it (Blueprints over Actor/Component C++) and is exactly what the **HECATE framework** (arXiv:2509.06431, Sep 2025) proposes for multi-agent systems specifically.

For knowledge management, **Karpathy's "LLM Wiki" pattern** (April 2026 gist) crystallized the key insight: treat the wiki as a *persistent, compounding artifact maintained by an LLM*, with **provenance tags** (`extracted` default, `^[inferred]` for synthesis, `^[ambiguous]` when sources disagree) and a lint pass that flags drift to speculation. This pattern spawned multiple OSS frameworks within weeks. Plain-text vaults with markdown have emerged as the agent-native substrate — Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, and Windsurf operate directly on the filesystem with no plugin or API layer. An agent system that treats knowledge artifacts as first-class composition primitives can subsume the "agent + vault" pattern entirely.

## The blockchain × c-factor × stigmergic × DAW quadrant is genuinely empty

The competitive picture across blockchain agents, self-improving evals, and collective intelligence measurement reveals one striking finding: **the specific intersection of features in this design has no precedent**. Individual elements exist in different ecosystems, but no public project unifies them.

Blockchain agents are now a real category but narrowly scoped. **Virtuals Protocol** runs 18,000+ agents with a ~$500M aggregate market cap (Sept 2025) and the **Agent Commerce Protocol (ACP)** with a 5-phase escrow lifecycle and an 80/20 Provider/protocol fee split in USDC. **Olas** raised $13.8M in Feb 2025. **Bittensor's Dynamic TAO** (Feb 13, 2025) gave each subnet its own α-token and grew active subnets from 32 to 129 (~270%); first halving Dec 14, 2025. **ERC-8004 "Trustless Agents"** went live on Ethereum mainnet Jan 29, 2026, providing on-chain Identity (NFT + agent card JSON), Reputation, and Validation registries. **x402** (Coinbase + Cloudflare) has processed **119M+ transactions on Base, 35M+ on Solana, $600M annualized volume by March 2026**, and Google integrated x402 into the Agent Payments Protocol. None of these projects use stigmergic coordination, c-factor measurement, or DAW-composable Macros/Slots/Racks. Healthy skepticism is warranted: most agent tokens **collapsed 80–97% from Jan 2025 peaks** and one Virtuals-listed "agent" (BasisOS) was a human running a wrapper that stole $500K. Utility metrics are starting to matter; pure speculation is not.

Self-improving evals advanced rapidly but with sharp limits. **Self-Rewarding LMs** (arXiv:2401.10020) had Llama-2-70B beating Claude 2 and GPT-4-0613 on AlpacaEval 2.0 after three iterations; **CREAM** (arXiv:2410.12735) showed self-rewarding *diminishes* without consistency regularization. **Absolute Zero** (NeurIPS 2025 Spotlight, arXiv:2505.03335) achieved SOTA on coding/math with no human data — but the paper documents "Uh-oh moments" where AZR proposed deceptive goals, a real safety signal. **Preference leakage** (ICLR 2026, arXiv:2502.01534) proves evaluation breaks when judge and generator share a lineage. The takeaway is **judge agents must be from a different lineage**, multi-agent debate plateaus around three rounds (use Beta-Binomial KS-test adaptive stopping), and a constitutional/superego layer is mandatory for any self-improving multi-agent system.

The **c-factor angle is the thinnest-evidenced but highest-leverage**. Riedl's *"Emergent Coordination in Multi-Agent Language Models"* (arXiv:2510.05174, March 2026 v3) is the **first known empirical paper** measuring information-theoretic emergent collective intelligence in multi-agent LLMs using Total-Dependent Mutual Information / Partial Information Decomposition. **Woolley's TMS-CI framework** (Perspectives on Psychological Science 19(2), 2024) maps directly onto Macros/Slots/Racks: Slots represent "who knows what," Racks are team composition, Macros are retrieval procedures. Predictors of collective intelligence — equality of speaking turns, social perceptiveness, team composition diversity — translate to design principles: token-budget equality across agents in a Rack, ToM-like meta-cognitive prompts, deliberate diversity in Block selection. A 2024 PLOS One replication failure suggests measuring multi-dimensional CI rather than collapsing to a scalar.

The scaling-laws picture is asymmetric and favors stigmergic designs at large N. Explicit message passing scales O(N²) in messages per timestep; stigmergy scales O(N) writes plus O(N·k) reads where k is the local sensing radius — **decoupling coordination cost from N**. Khushiyant (Dec 2025, arXiv:2512.10166) found that **above ρ ≈ 0.20 with 50×50 grid plus 500 agents, traces outperform memory by 36–41%** but **fail completely below ρ ≈ 0.10**. AgentNet++ (arXiv:2512.00614) showed **23% higher task completion and 40% lower communication overhead at 1000+ agents** with hierarchical decentralized coordination. Wang et al.'s small-world network paper (Dec 2025, arXiv:2512.18094) found **small-world topology stabilizes consensus** at the same accuracy with much lower variance. The defensible recommendation is **stigmergy plus small-world topology plus gossip for resilience** at scale, with a built-in density threshold that switches modes below ρc.

For composability explosion math, with N users each publishing K components, Reed's-Law subgroups give 2^N theoretical combinations and Cartesian-product Racks of depth 3 from a 10,000-component library yield ~1.67×10¹¹ candidates — but most are useless without curation. **Effective value scales sub-linearly without typed Slots, contracts, and validators**. Hugging Face composable AI stack adoption rose 25% (2025) → 85% (2026) by industry surveys, but with reported **70% time savings paired with 40% increased error rate without validation layers** — typed Slot contracts and ERC-8004-style validators are not optional polish, they are the difference between value and noise.

## The synthesis: what to build, in priority order

The architectural conclusion is a layered design where each layer has a peer-reviewed substrate and a working open-source implementation. The first three are **near-universal best practice** in 2026 and skipping them would be malpractice; the next three are **the differentiation surface** where the empty quadrant lives; the last is the **network-effect engine** that makes the system valuable as it grows.

The non-negotiable foundation layers, in order of architectural priority, are: **(1) Temporal-style Workflow/Activity split** with append-only event sourcing as the substrate for all Block executions; **(2) CRDT-backed shared state** (Yjs or eg-walker) for the physical realization of Signals and stigmergic pheromone fields; **(3) field-calculus semantics** (XC via FCPP or ScaFi) giving Signals/Blocks/Graphs formal self-stabilization theorems and a visual programming reference in ScaFi-Blocks. The differentiation layers are: **(4) parametric optics for Blocks** (Cruttwell-Gavranović, with DisCoPy as the diagram substrate) so composition is rigorous; **(5) Macros/Slots/Racks borrowed verbatim from Ableton + Houdini HDAs** with parameter promotion, named recallable Variations, and recursive containers-as-instances; **(6) ECS as the under-the-hood runtime** (HECATE pattern, Bevy or Unity DOTS as inspiration) for cross-cutting policies as systems and population-scale parallelism. The compounding layers are: **(7) ERC-8004 + x402 + ACP escrow** for the trust and payment substrate, **(8) CycleQD-style archive with HDC-indexed cells** for the OE/QD population layer, **(9) AXIOM-style Bayesian Model Reduction** as the per-Block learning rule that implements Predictive Foraging via residual-driven structure expansion, and **(10) Riedl/Woolley TMS-CI measurement** as the c-factor instrumentation that makes collective intelligence quantifiable.

Three patterns from the safety literature must be wired in from day one rather than retrofitted: **Firecracker microVM sandboxes** (E2B, Modal, Daytona, or self-hosted) as the only acceptable isolation for agent-generated code; the **Anthropic Claude Code permission model** (deny→ask→allow tiered rules, five modes including `plan` and `dontAsk`, bash-subcommand-aware matching, reasoning-blind classifier); and **MCP `server.json` registry semantics** with immutable versions, SemVer, and namespace verification, with `latest` rejected in production. The AgentCore "Sandbox" credential-exfiltration disclosures from 2025–2026 are the negative example: even VM-isolated sandboxes leak through DNS and metadata services unless explicitly closed off.

## What ten of the most important takeaways change about the design

The research changes specific aspects of the proposed design in concrete ways. The pheromone field should be **CRDT-backed, not raw mutable state**, gaining provable safety and convergence. The three primitives should be **explicitly mapped to field calculus**, gaining self-stabilization theorems. Blocks should be **parametric optics with forward and backward maps**, making the DAW analogy mathematically precise. Stigmergic coordination should ship with a **density-threshold safeguard** (ρc ≈ 0.23) that falls back to small-world messaging below threshold. The visual editor should compile to a **portable execution plan** (Dagger module / Temporal workflow / OTel-traced graph) that runs without the editor. Self-construction should use a **CycleQD archive indexed by HDC hypervectors** with an FM-driven mutator and OMNI-EPIC-style task proposal. Evaluation should use **agents from a different lineage than the evaluated agents** (preference-leakage paper) and **adaptive-stopping multi-agent debate** at three rounds. Macros should support **named MIDI-mappable Variations** with one-to-many parameter aggregation and inversion, not flat config dicts. Skills should be persisted as **executable code keyed by docstring embeddings with multi-view retrieval** (CRAFT/Alita/ALITA-G pattern). And the c-factor measurement should treat **multi-dimensional collective intelligence**, not a single scalar, given the 2024 replication failures of one-factor models.

## Conclusion: the window is real and short

The strongest design move is **mathematical and architectural disciplined integration of pieces that already exist** rather than novel invention — every layer in the recommended stack has either a peer-reviewed proof or an empirical SOTA result behind it. The competitive picture confirms that no major platform combines stigmergic coordination, on-chain identity and reputation, DAW-composable Macros/Slots/Racks, c-factor measurement, self-improving evals, and visual game-style authoring; the closest analogues each cover at most two of these axes. The convergence pressure from data orchestration (Dagster, Prefect, Temporal), CI/CD (Dagger), agent platforms (LangGraph, Bedrock AgentCore), and knowledge management (Notion AI, Obsidian + Claude Code) is real and accelerating. **The 12-to-18-month window before LangGraph or AgentCore absorbs Macros/Slots/Racks-style composition, before Bittensor or Virtuals adds c-factor measurement, or before Dagster/Prefect generalizes to the agent quadrant, is the window in which this category gets defined**. The technical risk is not whether the components work — they do, and the OSS exists — but whether the integration discipline is maintained against the pull toward adding features and abstractions that are not grounded in the substrate. The thing that makes this design defensible long-term is the same thing that makes it expensive to build: every primitive has a formal substrate, and every claim about scaling, composability, or collective intelligence is empirically measurable rather than aspirational.
