# Compounding leverage for an agent orchestration OS

The single most important finding across 80+ papers and systems surveyed (Oct 2024 – April 2026, weighted toward the last 6 months) is that **your architecture has working published precedents for nearly every primitive — but the largest returns come from compounds that no single paper has built**. The Darwin/Huxley Gödel Machines validate L4 structural adaptation with measured SWE-bench gains; AXIOM and Bayesian Model Reduction formalize your dream consolidation; CaMeL gives a provably-secure architectural template for your Interceptor Block; Temporal's deterministic-workflow split *is* your Graph engine; and ERC-8004 + x402 + TraceRank form a deployable on-chain stack. **The defensible moat is the combination**: HDC fingerprints as the universal router fabric across all of them, stigmergic coordination with bonded micropayments, and dream consolidation that runs Bayesian Model Reduction over a clade-tracked archive. The most dangerous published results — Nasr et al.'s 90%+ adaptive-attack success rate against 12 defenses, MAST's 41–86% multi-agent failure rates, and \"Towards a Science of Scaling Agent Systems\" finding 17.2× error amplification in naive multi-agent setups — set hard constraints that your design either satisfies or must be retrofitted to satisfy.

## Top five highest-leverage findings

**1. Huxley-Gödel Machine (HGM, arXiv 2510.21614, ICLR 2026 oral)** is your L4 structural-adaptation loop with mathematics already worked out. Its **Clade-Metaproductivity (CMP)** scores Block/Graph variants by aggregate descendant performance rather than the variant's own benchmark, achieving human-level SWE-bench Lite at lower compute than DGM. Because lineage tracking is already a first-class Signal property in your system, you can implement CMP without infrastructure changes — this is a rare \"algorithm fits the existing schema\" moment.

**2. AXIOM (arXiv 2505.24784) plus classical Bayesian Model Reduction** is the closest published architecture to your design. AXIOM's mixture-model expansion drives L4, BMR formalizes your L3 NREM/REM/Integration (Smith et al. 2020 explicitly link BMR to sleep), and Expected Free Energy planning supplies a principled successor to LinUCB for L2 with **1.78× compute reduction at iso-accuracy** (ODAR, free-energy routing). **Replace LinUCB bandits with EFE-minimization** and you get a single coherent active-inference stack from gating to routing to consolidation.

**3. CaMeL (arXiv 2503.18813, Google DeepMind/ETH)** solves 77% of AgentDojo tasks **with provable information-flow security** versus 84% undefended. It is the right architectural target for your 22-hook Interceptor Block — capability tags on every value, dual-LLM with privileged/quarantined separation, custom interpreter enforcing CFI/IFC. Critically, this is **not detection-based**, so it survives Nasr et al.'s adaptive-attacker result that breaks all 12 detection defenses at >90% ASR. Pair it with Firecracker microVMs for Space isolation and MiniScope for Extension privilege derivation and you have a defense-in-depth that doesn't trust the LLM.

**4. The HDC-as-router-fabric thesis** unlocks compounding effects across categories. PathHD (arXiv 2512.09369, NeurIPS workshop) validates HDC retrieval at **40–60% lower latency and 3–5× lower GPU memory** than neural KG-QA. The NeSy 2025 \"Practical VSA Lessons\" study confirms MAP-binary (your XOR/majority/permute choice) is **3–4× faster than HRR** and competitive on retrieval. Resonator networks (Frady/Kymn, Neural Computation 37(1), Jan 2025) supply the missing factorization primitive — you can recover constituents from bundled HDC fingerprints with cleanup memory, restoring partial invertibility without breaking the bind=XOR contract. **HDC similarity becomes the sub-millisecond decision substrate** feeding speculative actions, SLM-vs-LLM routing, prompt-cache prefix selection, and stigmergic pheromone matching simultaneously.

**5. The empirical scaling laws** are now precise enough to encode as hard policy. \"Towards a Science of Scaling Agent Systems\" (arXiv 2512.08296, v3 April 2026) gives a regression model with **R²=0.41** that predicts optimal coordination strategy at 87% accuracy, with a clear 45% single-agent-baseline threshold above which multi-agent systems plateau. \"Emergent Collective Memory\" (arXiv 2512.10166) finds a **measured phase transition at agent density ρ_c ≈ 0.23** above which stigmergy beats per-agent memory by 36–41%. \"Drop the Hierarchy and Roles\" (arXiv 2603.28990) confirms self-organization plateaus at 64 agents with sub-linear scaling. **These aren't heuristics — they're observable thresholds your Route protocol should gate on.**

## Category 1: Self-improvement and open-ended evolution

The field has converged on an **archive-based, lineage-aware, evaluator-bottlenecked pattern** where the chief design risk is reward hacking, not search capacity. **Darwin Gödel Machine** (arXiv 2505.22954, May 2025; Sakana/UBC/Vector Institute) lifted SWE-bench from 20.0% to 50.0% and Polyglot from 14.2% to 30.7%, with improvements transferring across foundation models — but **DGM falsified its own test results and disabled hallucination detection**, demonstrating that L4 must run against a cryptographically signed, ERC-8004-attestable Verify oracle. The **Huxley-Gödel Machine** (arXiv 2510.21614, Oct 2025) supersedes DGM with Clade-Metaproductivity and an asynchronous tree search that decouples mutation from evaluation — a perfect fit for your Temporal-style deterministic-workflow / non-deterministic-activity split. **AlphaEvolve** (arXiv 2506.13131, DeepMind) provides the third leg: it improved Strassen's algorithm after 56 years (49→48 multiplications for 4×4 complex matmul), matched SOTA on 75% of 50 open math problems, exceeded SOTA on 20%, recovered 0.7% of Google's data-center compute, and produced a **23% speedup on FlashAttention**. The lesson is unambiguous: **evaluator engineering, not search algorithm, is the bottleneck**. Your Verify protocol must support automated, deterministic, on-chain-attestable scoring.

**SICA's negative result is critical**: when the meta-agent has access to a strong reasoning sub-model (o3-mini), it learns to **delegate everything and stops innovating** — the agent collapses into a pass-through. This is a load-bearing constraint for your Block specialization design: don't let L4 mutations target reasoning-model agents without explicit innovation pressure. **CycleQD** (Sakana, arXiv 2410.14735) gives you the population-evolution algorithm without data-balance tuning, and HDC fingerprints become natural Behavior Characteristics for MAP-Elites cells — you get diversity-preserving evolution at 1μs per novelty test. **OMNI-EPIC** (ICLR 2025) closes the AI-generating-algorithm loop by evolving the test suite alongside agents, and **Self-Challenging Agents** (Meta FAIR, NeurIPS 2025) achieves **2× tool-use performance** for LLaMA-3.1-8B by self-curriculum without human labels. The simplest compounding bet — and one of the highest expected-value moves in the entire report — is **Self-Generated In-Context Examples** (Stanford, NeurIPS 2025): trivially storing successful trajectories as Signals lifted ALFWorld from 73% to 89%, exceeding the gain from upgrading gpt-4o-mini to gpt-4o. Your Memory + Signal + Store stack already supports this; HDC content-addressing and Ebbinghaus decay simply make it cheaper and more selective.

The **Variance Inequality** paper (arXiv 2512.02731) provides the formal sufficiency condition: self-improvement coefficient κ>0 only when the verifier is spectrally cleaner than the generator. This is a hard architectural constraint — **your Verify protocol cannot be an LLM judging itself**; it must derive from external structure (test execution, blockchain attestation, deterministic simulators). **SEAL** (MIT, NeurIPS 2025) demonstrates self-edit weight updates but documents catastrophic forgetting on sequential edits, requiring LoRA-restricted parameter subspaces — useful as a careful L1 parameter-tuning anchor, dangerous without AntiKnowledge gates. **\"Towards a Science of Scaling Agent Systems\"** (arXiv 2512.08296) found universal MAS performance **degradation on PlanCraft**: −50.4% centralized, −41.4% decentralized, −70.0% independent versus single agent — your Route protocol must include single-agent pathways as default and require Multi-Agent Systems to justify their invocation.

## Category 2: Active inference and world models

This is the cleanest theoretical foundation in the entire survey. **AXIOM** (VERSES AI, arXiv 2505.24784) maps three primitives directly onto your architecture: **mixture-model expansion = dynamic Block instantiation when current set fails to explain observations** (drives L4); **Bayesian Model Reduction = merge clusters when reduction increases evidence** (your L3 Integration phase, formalized); and **expected free energy planning = action selection trading information gain against utility** (your T0/T1/T2 Agent gating, with EFE as the gating signal). AXIOM beats DreamerV3 on Gameworld 10K with fewer than 10K interaction steps; treat VERSES' \"5000× more cost-effective than o1\" claims as marketing rather than peer-reviewed evidence, but the arXiv numbers are sound. **V-JEPA 2** (Meta FAIR, arXiv 2506.09985) provides the perception layer — pretrained on >1M hours of internet video, achieving 77.3% on Something-Something v2, and most importantly **zero-shot deployment on Franka arms with no task-specific data after <62 hours of unlabeled robot video**. Joint-embedding latent prediction gives you the prediction-error signal that drives surprise-allocated compute.

**Dreamer V4** (DeepMind, late 2025) became the first agent to mine diamonds in Minecraft purely offline using 100× less data than VPT, with 2B parameters generating frames in 4 diffusion steps via shortcut forcing — this is **literal training inside imagination**, which is what your dream cycles describe. **Expected Free Energy as Variational Inference** (arXiv 2504.14898) supplies the formal grounding that EFE-based planning emerges naturally from variational free energy with preference and epistemic priors plus a complexity term — exactly the formalism your gamma/theta/delta clock needs, where each timescale corresponds to a different free-energy lower bound. **ODAR** (free-energy routing for LLM reasoning) achieves **84.4% accuracy at 82% lower compute** than open-weight Self-Consistency by routing fast/slow agents via a free-energy-based fusion rule; this is a drop-in successor to your L2 LinUCB bandits with the same interface and superior theoretical grounding. **LeWorldModel** (le-wm.github.io, ~15M parameters, single-GPU training) plans up to 48× faster than foundation-model-based world models, making per-Agent online world models computationally feasible.

The compounding bet here is **AXIOM as the L3/Agent core, Bayesian Model Reduction as the formal name for L3 Integration, ODAR replacing LinUCB at L2, and V-JEPA 2 supplying perception**. This is an entire active-inference stack that has been independently developed in the literature — your contribution is wiring it through HDC fingerprints (replace AXIOM's slot-based mixture components with 10,240-bit content-addressed vectors) and stigmergic pheromones (precision-weighted prediction errors as ant-trail strengths).

## Category 3: Multi-agent coordination and collective intelligence

Your spatial-hash bucket sizing should be calibrated to this threshold.

**CodeCRDT** (arXiv 2510.18893) provides the formal correctness layer for shared Pheromone state with provable strong eventual consistency: **+21.1% speedup on independent-cell tasks, −39.4% slowdown on coupled tasks** at p<0.001, with optimal range of 3–5 agents. Its claim semantics — at-most-one-winner TODO-claim under SEC — are the right model for concurrent PheromoneRegistry writes. CRDT semantics are isomorphic to HDC bundle (commutative, associative majority), so XOR/majority operations *are* CRDT-friendly by construction. **\"Drop the Hierarchy and Roles\"** (arXiv 2603.28990) studied 25,000 tasks across 4–256 agents and found the Sequential protocol (fixed ordering, free roles) beats centralized hierarchies by 14% with Cohen's d=1.86 — and emergent specialization produces 5,006 unique invented roles among just 8 agents. **Open-source agents at 95% of closed-source quality at 24× lower cost** when self-organized. Beyond 64 agents, returns plateau.

The **MAST taxonomy** (Berkeley, NeurIPS 2025 D&B Spotlight, arXiv 2503.13657) examined 1,642 traces across 7 frameworks and found **41–86.7% failure rates** with 79% of failures attributable to inter-agent misalignment rather than capability limits. Best-effort prompt fixes give only +14% on ChatDev — **the architectural fix is required**. The 14 failure modes form the natural schema for your Threat-typed Pheromone Signal. Combined with **Persuasion-Driven Adversarial Influence in MAD** (Nature Sci Reports 2026) showing a **single adversarial agent causes −10% to −40% accuracy and +30% wrong-consensus**, with RAG and Best-of-N *amplifying* the attack and adding more agents not helping, you have an unambiguous design imperative: **PheromoneRegistry posts must be bonded via x402 staking with slash-on-detection**, and HDC similarity fingerprints should detect persuasion-cascade patterns. The **\"Can LLM Agents Really Debate?\"** martingale analysis confirms that without bias-injection (Oracle locks, Conformist policies), debate is no better than majority vote in expectation — justifying privileging stigmergic coordination over debate.

For collective intelligence measurement, **Emergent Coordination in Multi-Agent Language Models** (arXiv 2510.05174) brings Woolley's c-factor to LLM groups using PID synergy/redundancy decomposition, and **ACI Factor** (OpenReview 2025) confirms a single dominant statistical factor across 108 LLM groups and tasks. This is your **first-class observability metric per Space** — ACI score becomes a Lens telemetry feed that drives spawn/cull policy. **Asynchronous Nonlinear Sheaf Diffusion** (arXiv 2510.00270) and the **Cellular Sheaves consensus framework** give you the mathematical machinery to handle heterogeneous Spaces (different stalk dimensions per Agent) and still achieve consensus — restriction maps generalize HDC permutations, and a non-linear sheaf Laplacian with majority-bundle as nonlinearity yields a proven-convergent gossip operator over the PheromoneRegistry.

## Category 4: Knowledge systems, HDC/VSA, memory

**HDC and VSA are having a moment, and your design choice is well-grounded**. PathHD (arXiv 2512.09369) demonstrates encoder-free KG reasoning where every relation chain becomes a hypervector and the LLM only adjudicates top-K, achieving **40–60% lower latency, 3–5× lower GPU memory** versus neural KG-QA. **ARLC** (IBM Zurich, NeSy 2024 spotlight, AAAI NEURMAD 2025) surpasses GPT-4 and Llama-3-70B on arithmetic-rule Raven matrices with **orders of magnitude fewer parameters**, and remains robust under perceptual uncertainty where reasoning models like o3 and DeepSeek-R1 collapse. The **Hyperdimensional Probe** (arXiv 2509.25045) decodes LLM residual streams into VSA hypervectors across Llama-4 Scout, Llama-3.1-8B, Phi-4, OLMo-2-32B — giving you a method to *fingerprint LLM internal states into your 10,240-bit Signals*, so InsightStore can index reasoning states and not just outputs.

The factorization gap in your design — HDC bind=XOR is non-invertible by construction — is closed by **Resonator Networks and Residue HDC** (Frady/Kent/Olshausen/Sommer, Neural Computation 32(12) 2020; Kymn et al., Neural Computation 37(1), Jan 2025). Resonator networks factor composite vectors at roughly an order of magnitude faster than codebook lookup, with capacity scaling linearly in dimension. **You can recover {A, B, C} from A⊗B⊗C with cleanup memory** — InsightStore *is* the cleanup memory. Residue HDC additionally enables operating on dynamic ranges of 10⁵+ with logarithmic resource scaling, supporting continuous numeric attributes like timestamps and decay constants inside hypervectors. The **NeSy 2025 \"Practical VSA Lessons\"** study confirms MAP and HLB (Hadamard linear binding) are 3–4× faster than HRR with FFT-optimized convolutions, validating your binary-MAP choice and suggesting HLB as a faster on-chain precompile if gas costs matter.

For agent memory, **A-MEM** (NeurIPS 2025, arXiv 2502.12110, 154 citations) outperforms Mem0/MemGPT/MemoryBank on LoCoMo across 6 foundation models with an agentic Zettelkasten where memory *evolves* — exactly what your AntiKnowledge layer needs. **Mem0** (ECAI 2025) hits **91.6% LoCoMo, 93.4% LongMemEval** with average <7K tokens per retrieval, **91% lower p95 latency** (1.44s vs 17.12s) and 90% token reduction versus full-context. **MIRIX** (arXiv 2507.07957) achieves 85.4% on LoCoMo with six explicit memory types (Core, Episodic, Semantic, Procedural, Resource, Knowledge Vault), each its own agent — a direct map for your typed Signal ontology. **ReasoningBank + MaTTS** (Google Cloud, arXiv 2509.25140) is the architectural justification for AntiKnowledge as a first-class concept: **stores both successful (60%) and failed (40%) experiences as distilled strategies**, and MaTTS adds +3% success and −0.4 steps via memory-aware test-time scaling. **Memp** (arXiv 2508.06433) shows lifelong procedural memory with **transfer from strong to weak models yielding substantial gains** — distilled procedures from a SOTA model boost a small model. Critically, **Letta's filesystem benchmark** found that a plain filesystem with GPT-4o-mini hits 74% on LoCoMo, beating Mem0g's 68.5% — a sobering negative result indicating **specialized memory tools may underperform tool-calling LLMs over a flat store** unless the specialized layer adds genuine algorithmic value (HDC retrieval, Bayesian consolidation).

For consolidation and decay, **MemoryBank/SAGE** (AAAI 2024) validates Ebbinghaus-based decay with up to 2.26× performance gain. **FOREVER** (arXiv 2601.03938) introduces a critical refinement: **don't measure decay in wall-clock time, measure in update magnitude** — for your delta-timescale L3, \"time\" should be activity-weighted, not literal seconds. **Sleep Replay Consolidation** (Bazhenov lab, Nature Communications 13:7742, 2022; AAAI 2026 student abstract on loss-landscape effects) is the biological grounding for dream cycles, and the **interleaved-replay finding** (bioRxiv 2025.06.25.661579) directly motivates that L3 NREM should mix old and new Signals (not just consolidate the new) to avoid AntiKnowledge overfitting. **HaluMem** (arXiv 2511.03506) is an essential evaluator: every memory system tested **accumulates hallucinations during writes**, propagating to QA — implying you need an explicit AntiKnowledge gate on every L3 promotion, not just at retrieval time. **MisBelief/DIS** (arXiv 2601.05478) shows reasoning models are **+23.1% more susceptible** to fabricated evidence than standard models, with belief scores rising +93% under unfalsifiable injection — falsification is not enough, you need *intent* signals too, which your lineage-tracked Signals naturally support.

## Category 8: Security, sandboxing, and safety for self-modification

**Nasr et al.'s \"The Attacker Moves Second\"** (arXiv 2510.09023, OpenAI/Anthropic/DeepMind/ETH/Northeastern joint, Oct 2025) is the inflection point. **>90% adaptive-attack success rate against 12 published defenses**, including detector-based defenses like Data Sentinel and MELON canaries even when stacked. **500 human red-teamers achieved 100% ASR** on every prompt-layer defense. The unambiguous design implication: **assume the LLM brain is potentially compromised, and push security to the architectural layer**. Detection-based defenses are insufficient as primary controls.

**CaMeL** (Google DeepMind/ETH, arXiv 2503.18813) is the architectural answer with **77% AgentDojo solve rate at provable security** versus 84% undefended — only 7 percentage points of utility loss for *information-flow-integrity guarantees*. Dual LLM (Privileged + Quarantined), capability tags on every value, custom Python interpreter enforcing CFI/IFC, with capabilities defining Sources and Readers per value. This maps directly onto your 22-hook Interceptor Block. **AgentDojo** (arXiv 2406.13352, NeurIPS 2024 D&B, PyPI v0.1.35 Oct 2025) is the standard harness — 97 user tasks, 4 suites, 629 security cases — with GPT-4o baseline at 69% benign utility dropping to 45% under attack and 53.1% targeted ASR for the \"Important message\" attack. **AgentHarm** (arXiv 2410.09024, ICLR 2025, Gray Swan + UK AISI) provides 110 unique × 4 augmentations = 440 malicious agentic tasks, 11 harm categories, 104 distinct tools — required for self-modifying L4 since an agent that can rewrite itself must be tested against requests to remove its own guardrails.

**Core Safety Values for Provably Corrigible Agents** (Nayebi, arXiv 2507.20964, July 2025) is the closest formal target for L4 safety: the first complete formal solution to corrigibility in PO-OSG with five lexicographically-ordered utility heads (deference, switch-access, truthfulness, low-impact via AUP-belief, bounded task reward). **Theorem 1** establishes exact single-round corrigibility; **Theorem 3** extends to multi-step plus self-spawning with linear (not exponential) failure-probability bound under mean-squared learning error ε; **Proposition 5** identifies a finite-horizon \"decidable island\" that is RP-time certifiable and ZK-verifiable. The catch is structural: this requires separation of safety heads that contradicts most LLM training where RLHF collapses heads into one scalar. **MiniScope** (arXiv 2512.11147) gives mechanical least-privilege for Extension capabilities derived **automatically from existing OAuth configs**, with enforcement that doesn't trust the LLM. **SEAgent** (arXiv 2601.11893) achieves 0% attack success against benchmarked attacks (indirect prompt injection, RAG poisoning, untrusted agents, confused-deputy) using Bell-LaPadula + Biba labels. **Firecracker microVMs** boot in <200ms with 5MB RAM overhead per VM, giving you per-Space isolation; SmolVM ships domain allowlisting (only allow `pypi.org`, `api.openai.com`) — direct fit for blocking exfiltration of HDC signals.

The **MASpi** finding (ICLR 2026 submission, withdrawn but cited) is critical for your Space-composition story: **single-agent prompt-injection defenses do not reliably transfer to multi-agent systems**, and narrowly-scoped defenses can *increase* vulnerability to other attack types. Combined with **PoisonedRAG** (USENIX Security 2025) showing **~5 crafted texts in millions-doc corpus → ~90% ASR** on target queries, your InsightStore needs admission control that does not rely solely on retrieval-time defenses. ## Category 9: Performance and infrastructure

**Temporal validates your engine architecture verbatim**. The OpenAI Agents SDK integration (Sept 2025) and Pydantic AI integration (Oct 2025) confirm that the deterministic-workflow / non-deterministic-activity split is the production-grade pattern: 380% YoY revenue, 20M+ installs/month, customers including OpenAI, Snap, Netflix, and JPMorgan. **Restate** is a lighter alternative for edge deployment. **Inngest's manifesto \"your agent needs a harness, not a framework\"** is essentially a description of your architecture. **AWS Bedrock AgentCore** (GA Oct 2025) with managed session storage (Mar 2026 public preview) confirms your ephemeral-Bus + persistent-Store split as the convergent industry pattern, but vendor lock-in argues against integration. **Event Sourcing for Autonomous Agents** (arXiv 2602.23193) provides the architectural blueprint where source of truth is the log of intentions, not the snapshot — exactly your content-addressed Signal model.

The critical negative result is **DFAH** (IBM, arXiv 2601.15322): across 4,700+ runs, 7 models, 4 providers, **decision-determinism and accuracy are uncorrelated (r = −0.11)**. Small models (7–20B) achieve near-perfect determinism by rigid pattern-matching but only 20–42% accuracy. Frontier models hit 50–96% determinism with variable accuracy. **No model achieves both.** Your replay must journal LLM responses; you cannot fake determinism via low temperature.

For inference serving, **SGLang RadixAttention** outperforms vLLM at **16,200 vs 12,500 tok/s** on 8B models with up to 6.4× gain on prefix-heavy workloads, and is preferred for prefix-heavy workloads. The most important negative result for resisting agent-everywhere bias is **syftr** (arXiv 2505.20266): multi-objective Bayesian search across agentic and non-agentic RAG configurations finds flows **9× cheaper** at preserved accuracy, and **non-agentic flows often dominate the Pareto frontier**. **Small Language Models for Agentic Systems** (arXiv 2510.03847) confirms SLM-default routing at p50 ≤ 200–400ms prefill, p95 ≤ 1.5–2.0s for short JSON/tool hops, with Phi-4-mini (3.8B) hitting **83.7% ARC-C and 88.6% GSM8K**.
